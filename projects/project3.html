Distillery Automation Modernization –
Legacy PLC Migration & Control Panel Rehabilitation for a Food & Beverage Processing Facility

Location: Philippines | Completed
Project Overview

Watchdog Automation successfully executed a complete automation modernization project for a major Food & Beverage distillation facility, replacing a legacy control platform with a high-performance Siemens automation architecture. The project addressed the challenges of obsolete hardware, limited system support, and aging control infrastructure, ensuring long-term operational reliability and improved plant performance.

The existing automation system was built on the Allen-Bradley SLC500 platform, which had reached obsolescence and posed increasing risks related to spare parts availability, system downtime, and maintenance limitations. Our engineering team carried out a full migration of the control system to the Siemens SIMATIC S7-1500 platform, providing a modern, scalable, and future-ready automation solution.

Software Migration

A major part of the project involved reverse engineering and software migration. The original RSLogix 500 program was carefully analyzed, re-structured, and converted into Siemens TIA Portal V20, ensuring that all process logic, interlocks, and safety conditions were accurately replicated while taking advantage of the advanced capabilities of the new platform.

Operator Interface Upgrade

To complement the PLC upgrade, the operator interface was modernized using a Siemens SIMATIC Unified Comfort Panel (MTP1200). The new HMI provides enhanced visualization, improved diagnostics, and a significantly more intuitive user experience for plant operators and maintenance personnel.

Control Panel Rehabilitation

Beyond the control system upgrade, the project included a complete rehabilitation of the automation control panel. The legacy enclosure and internal electrical components were replaced with a new industrial-grade SS316 stainless steel enclosure, specifically designed for the harsh environmental conditions typically present in distillation and food processing environments.

All internal components — including power distribution devices, relays, terminal blocks, protection devices, and wiring systems — were replaced with new industrial-grade hardware, resulting in a fully rebuilt control panel compliant with modern industrial standards.

The final system now operates on a high-performance Siemens S7-1517 PLC, providing substantial improvements in processing power, communication capability, diagnostics, and long-term maintainability.

Project Highlights
Migration from Allen-Bradley SLC500 to Siemens SIMATIC S7-1500 CPU 1517
Conversion of RSLogix 500 program to Siemens TIA Portal V20 architecture
Deployment of Siemens MTP1200 Unified HMI for advanced plant visualization
Complete control panel rehabilitation and modernization
Installation of a SS316 stainless steel industrial enclosure for corrosion resistance
Replacement of all internal electrical components and wiring
Improved system reliability, maintainability, and spare parts availability
Engineering Value Delivered

This project demonstrates Watchdog Automation's capability to perform complex cross-platform PLC migrations, modernize legacy industrial systems, and deliver fully engineered control solutions aligned with current automation standards.

The upgraded system now provides the distillery with:

Higher automation reliability
Enhanced operator visibility and diagnostics
Reduced maintenance risks associated with obsolete equipment
Improved scalability for future process expansion
